Introduction

YouTube Music doesn't have a native "listening party" feature like Spotify, but with a little creativity, you can still enjoy music with your friends in real-time. This guide will walk you through the best methods to achieve this.

Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you and your friends have the following:

  • A YouTube Music Premium subscription (for uninterrupted listening).
  • A voice chat application like Discord or Telegram.
  • A stable internet connection.

Step-by-Step Setup

The most reliable method is to use a shared playlist and a voice call to synchronize playback.

1. Create a Shared Playlist

First, create a new playlist in YouTube Music and add the songs you want to listen to. Then, share the playlist with your friends.

2. Start a Voice Call

Get everyone on a voice call in your preferred application. This is crucial for communication and synchronization.

3. Synchronize Playback

Designate one person as the "DJ". The DJ will count down ("3, 2, 1, play!") and everyone will press the play button at the same time. This simple method is surprisingly effective.

Troubleshooting

If you run into issues, here are a few things to try:

  • Playback out of sync: Pause the music and re-synchronize using the countdown method.
  • Buffering issues: Check your internet connection. Lowering the audio quality in YouTube Music settings can also help.

Tips & Tricks

Here are some extra tips to enhance your listening party:

  • Use a Discord bot to play music directly in a voice channel.
  • Share your screen to show the current song and music video.
  • Create a collaborative playlist and let everyone add songs.
